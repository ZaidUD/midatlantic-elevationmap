Transformative Mid-Atlantic Elevation Dataset

Canopy Height Model and DEM at 1-meter resolution

Kevin R. Roa, Fabian Perez, Nestor F. Porras-Diaz, Gonzalo R. Arce
Computational Imaging and Machine Learning Laboratory
Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering
University of Delaware

A geo-referenced 1-meter resolution dataset of canopy height models (CHM) across the Mid-Atlantic would represent a transformative resource for both science and applied management. At this resolution, features such as individual trees, field edges, riparian buffers, tidal creeks, and parcel-scale land uses become visible, enabling analyses at the scale where ecological, hydrological, and human processes interact. For science, this means stronger habitat models, improved carbon accounting, and early detection of invasive species. For policy and practice, it means actionable insights for Mid-Atlantic restoration efforts, urban tree canopy equity, precision agriculture, and resilience planning against sea-level rise and flooding. Critically, region-wide 1-meter coverage ensures consistent, cross-jurisdictional baselines that fuel machine-learning models, enable time-series change detection, and support collaboration between states and agencies. The result is a high-fidelity, decision-ready dataset that bridges fundamental science and practical applications in climate resilience, biodiversity conservation, infrastructure planning, and risk management.

Dataset Coverage

This dataset provides Canopy Height Maps over the mid-atlantic area. Particularly covering the states:

  • Delaware
